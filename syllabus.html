Course Syllabus

Semester: I   |   Credits: 4   |   Total Hours: 60

Course Objective

To enhance the skills to effectively shape organizational behaviour, implement successful change strategies, foster innovation, manage digital transitions, and build inclusive, adaptable, and sustainable work environments.

Module Breakdown

  • Module 1: Organizational Culture Dynamics
    Organizational culture in shaping behaviour and decision-making, levels of culture, leadership roles, assessing culture and climate, subcultures, digitalization impact, Schein and Deal & Kennedy models, teamwork and collaboration.
  • Module 2: Change Management Models
    Change process, drivers, leadership challenges, models – Lewin, Kotter, McKinsey 7S, ADKAR, Bridges, Competing Values, strategies for implementation.
  • Module 3: Building Digital Culture
    Technology and AI impact on culture, stakeholder engagement, culture audits and mapping, cross-functional collaboration, addressing cultural barriers.
  • Module 4: Agile Change Management
    Digital-era adaptations, KPIs, ethical transformation, addressing resistance, agile frameworks, institutionalizing change.
  • Module 5: Trends and Practices
    DEIB, hybrid work, data-driven and innovation cultures, sustainability integration, CSR, automation and cultural evolution.

Course Outcomes (CO)

  1. Analyze the impact of organizational culture on behavior, decision-making, and digital transformation using cultural frameworks.
  2. Interpret change management models and strategies, focusing on leadership roles.
  3. Assess the role of technology in shaping digital culture and overcoming cultural barriers.
  4. Compare agile change management strategies and address ethical and resistance challenges.
  5. Demonstrate trends in DEIB, hybrid work, and sustainability-driven cultures.
