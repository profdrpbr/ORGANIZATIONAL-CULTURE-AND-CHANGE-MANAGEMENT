Welcome

Welcome to the course Organizational Culture and Change Management. This subject explores how leadership, culture, and digital transformation shape organizations. You’ll engage with frameworks, case studies, and models that prepare you to lead purposeful and adaptable enterprises.

Course Syllabus

Course Code: —
Credits: 4 | Hours: 60

Course Objective

To enhance the skills to effectively shape organizational behaviour, implement successful change strategies, foster innovation, manage digital transitions, and build inclusive, adaptable, and sustainable work environments.

Module Overview

  • Module 1: Organizational Culture Dynamics – Elements, leadership role, digitalization, frameworks (Schein, Deal & Kennedy), teamwork.
  • Module 2: Change Management Models – Lewin, Kotter, McKinsey 7-S, ADKAR, Bridges’ Model; leadership and strategies.
  • Module 3: Building Digital Culture – technology, AI impact, cultural audits, stakeholder engagement, cross-functional collaboration.
  • Module 4: Agile Change Management – digital-era adaptation, KPIs, ethical transformation, resistance management.
  • Module 5: Trends & Practices – DEIB, hybrid work, innovation-driven culture, sustainability, CSR, automation.

Course Outcomes (CO)

  1. Analyze the impact of organizational culture on behavior, decision-making, and digital transformation using cultural frameworks.
  2. Interpret change management models and strategies focusing on leadership roles.
  3. Assess the role of technology in shaping digital culture and overcoming cultural barriers.
  4. Compare agile change strategies and address ethical, digital, and resistance issues.
  5. Demonstrate trends in DEIB, hybrid work, and sustainability within change management.
